About the Dataset

The Breast Cancer Wisconsin (Diagnostic) Dataset was created by Dr. William H. Wolberg at the University of Wisconsin. It contains features computed from digitized images of fine needle aspirate (FNA) biopsies of breast masses.

Features describe characteristics of the cell nuclei present in the image, based on the work of W.N. Street, W.H. Wolberg, and O.L. Mangasarian (1993).

Samples569
Features30 numeric
Classes2 (M / B)
Missing valuesNone

The 10 Core Features (×3 statistics)

Each of the 10 nucleus characteristics is measured as mean, standard error, and worst (largest), giving 30 total features.

1. Radius — mean distances from center to points on the perimeter
2. Texture — standard deviation of gray-scale values
3. Perimeter
4. Area
5. Smoothness — local variation in radius lengths
6. Compactness — perimeter² / area − 1.0
7. Concavity — severity of concave portions of the contour
8. Concave points — number of concave portions
9. Symmetry
10. Fractal dimension — coastline approximation − 1
